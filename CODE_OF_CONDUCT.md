# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want GaiaLog to be a professional, welcoming, and constructive project for maintainers, contributors, reviewers, and users.

## Expected behaviour

- be respectful and clear
- assume good intent, but focus on evidence and impact
- welcome feedback, correction, and review
- prioritise the health of the project over ego or point-scoring
- keep discussion technical, professional, and constructive

## Unacceptable behaviour

- harassment, abuse, intimidation, or discrimination
- bad-faith review, trolling, or personal attacks
- sharing private information without permission
- deliberately disruptive behaviour in issues, pull requests, or discussions

## Project standards

Because GaiaLog deals with production-style infrastructure and blockchain writes, contributors are expected to:

- avoid reckless or destructive suggestions
- avoid encouraging insecure shortcuts
- take security, data integrity, and operational impact seriously

## Enforcement

If you experience or observe unacceptable behaviour, contact the project maintainer privately through the repository's agreed contact channel.

Maintainers may remove comments, close threads, reject contributions, or restrict participation where necessary to protect the project and its contributors.
